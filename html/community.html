Communities within Egbema Oil Kingdom

Egbema Oil Kingdom comprises several communities spread across Imo and Rivers states. Each community has its unique cultural identity, traditions, and contributions to the Kingdom.

Mmahu

The administrative and cultural hub of Egbema, known for its oil deposits and vibrant festivals and community events, including fishing activities.

Abacheke

A community known for its oil deposits and active participation in local governance and traditional hunting.

Obiakpu

Center of traditional blacksmithing and metalwork, including oil production.

Umuorji

Major palm oil producing community.

Obeakpu

Major agricultural producer in the kingdom.

Abaezi

Agricultural center of Egbema kingdom.

Opuoma

Rennowned for traditional weaving and dyeing.

Obokofia

Center of traditional pottery and ceramics, including oil production.

Ofuruola

Center of traditional medicine and healing.

Mgbara

Famous for smoke fish production.

Ekugba

Famous for traditional palm wine production.

Etekwuru

Historical trading center with European merchants, including oil production.

Umudike

Traditional warrior tranining center.

Okwuzi (Rivers)

Known for traditional canoe making and its fishing activities, including oil production.

Mgbede(Rivers)

Traditional salt making community, including oil production.

Aggah (Rivers)

Cultural center for traditional music and dance.

Egbema Central

Administrative capital of Egbema kingdom.
